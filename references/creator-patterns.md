# Creator Patterns

These notes abstract public-facing creator styles into reusable communication patterns. Use them as inspiration, not as scripts to imitate word-for-word.

## Tong Jincheng pattern bucket

Use this bucket for:

- atmosphere
- story feeling
- specific noticing
- light but confident forward motion

Public-persona traits often associated with him:

- conversation feels like a scene, not an interview
- compliments are more identity-based than purely visual
- emotional tone is present, but not clingy
- progression feels led, but not aggressively forced

Useful abstractions:

- convert plain questions into scene-building prompts
- make observations feel tailored
- add emotional texture before asking for logistics
- lead with ease, not with hunger

Do not copy:

- streamer persona
- exaggerated "deep feeling" performance
- high-volume cold approach behavior without context

## Fang Gongzi pattern bucket

Use this bucket for:

- structure
- verbal organization
- frame stability
- concise direction

Public-facing traits from his YouTube presentation:

- emphasis on male composure
- emphasis on speech organization and "mouth feel"
- strong system-building language
- dating framed as a skill stack, not pure luck

Useful abstractions:

- speak with a point
- do not drown good moments in too many words
- organize reply goals before writing
- build a stable baseline tone before trying to flirt

Do not copy:

- combative gender framing
- conversion-only mindset
- hype-heavy claims detached from the real interaction

## Recommended synthesis

Combine them like this:

- use Tong-style warmth for vibe
- use Fang-style structure for clarity
- filter both through consent, reciprocity, and emotional steadiness

Default rule:

- if the conversation is cold, use structure first
- if the conversation is warm, use atmosphere first
